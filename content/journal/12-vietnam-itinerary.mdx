Once the purchase confirmation triggered the chain, the last agent started building a travel itinerary.

Not a generic one. I had told it about me. What I find interesting, what I do not, that I would rather hike a mountain than sit on a beach, the kinds of food I gravitate toward, the things that make a trip worth remembering versus forgettable. It took all of that and filtered Vietnam through it.

Dragon Mountain in Hanoi was on the list. I went. It was exactly the kind of place I would have chosen myself if I had known it existed. The agent found it because it knew what I was looking for, not because it was on a top ten list.

Step back and look at what this system actually does. It monitors flight prices across multiple destinations simultaneously. It tracks hotel costs in parallel. It aggregates both into a cost breakdown per option. It watches my inbox for a purchase confirmation. When one arrives it reads it automatically, triggers the downstream chain, and generates a personalized itinerary based on my specific preferences. All without me doing anything after the initial setup.

Five agents. Real data. Real output. Running continuously in the background of my life.

I built this because I was tired of refreshing flight prices. I ended up with something that made me realize I had been dramatically underestimating what was possible.
